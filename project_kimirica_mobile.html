1. Overview

The Kimirica website redesign lifted desktop conversion, but mobile web lagged noticeably behind it — enough that the business greenlit a native app aimed squarely at repeat buyers who already trusted the brand and just needed a faster, friendlier way to shop again.

2. Problem

Mobile web checkout required re-entering payment details on every single visit, had tap targets too small for comfortable one-handed use, and offered no meaningful way to re-engage a customer after their first purchase besides a generic marketing email.

Meanwhile, Kimirica's most loyal customers — the ones re-purchasing their favorite products every few months — had no faster path back into a repeat order than starting a full browse-and-search session from scratch each time.

3. Solution

I designed a native information architecture optimized for one-handed use, with biometric-authenticated checkout and saved payment methods removing the re-entry friction that plagued mobile web, plus reorder shortcuts built specifically for repeat buyers.

Fig 1. Biometric-authenticated checkout with saved payment methods, replacing repeated manual entry.

4. My Role

I owned the mobile IA and checkout redesign, and worked with the CRM/lifecycle marketing team to design a push notification strategy grounded in actual browsing and cart behavior rather than generic promotional blasts.

5. Key Features

Biometric Checkout

Face ID / fingerprint authentication paired with securely saved payment methods, reducing checkout to two taps for returning customers.

One-Tap Reorder

A dedicated "Buy Again" shelf on the home screen surfacing past purchases, letting loyal customers skip browsing entirely for routine restocks.

Behavior-Based Push Notifications

Re-engagement nudges tied to specific actions — an abandoned cart, a viewed-but-not-purchased product back in stock — instead of blanket promotional pushes.

6. Business Impact

+17% Mobile Checkout Completion
Higher Repeat Purchase Rate
Faster Time-to-Reorder
