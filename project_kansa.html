1. Overview

Kansa, an organic skincare brand, hired me as a freelance designer to help move the business from one-off purchases toward a subscription model — a common goal in D2C skincare, where repeat, predictable revenue matters more than any single sale.

2. Problem

The organic skincare category is crowded with near-identical-sounding products, and Kansa's storefront presented its full catalog with no guidance on which products suited which skin type or concern — leaving customers to guess, buy one item, and often never return.

The existing "subscribe and save 10%" toggle at checkout was rarely used, because a discount alone didn't answer the real question: would this product even keep working for my skin over time.

3. Solution

I designed a short skin-type quiz that generates a personalized routine recommendation, then framed the subscription around routine continuity — "your routine, refilled automatically" — rather than a generic percentage-off toggle.

Fig 1. Skin-type quiz output generating a personalized routine and subscription recommendation.

4. My Role

Working as the only designer on this engagement, I designed the quiz flow, the routine-recommendation logic in collaboration with the founder's product knowledge, and the subscription management screens, all within a focused four-week sprint.

5. Key Features

Skin-Type Quiz

A five-question quiz translating skin concerns into a personalized routine recommendation, replacing catalog browsing with a guided starting point.

Routine-Framed Subscription

Subscription positioned as "your routine, refilled automatically" rather than a discount checkbox, tying it to an ongoing outcome instead of a one-time saving.

Flexible Skip & Reschedule Controls

One-tap skip and reschedule options directly from the account screen, reducing the outright cancellations that rigid subscription schedules tend to cause.

6. Business Impact

Higher Subscription Attach Rate
Improved 90-Day Repeat Purchase Rate
Fewer Outright Cancellations
