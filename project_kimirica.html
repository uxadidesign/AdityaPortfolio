1. Project Overview

Background & Business Context

Kimirica Lifestyle is a premium D2C e-commerce platform specializing in luxury bath, body, and wellness products. E-commerce success is determined by micro-conversions in the shopping cart: a fraction of a percent difference in cart abandonments translates directly into millions of dollars in annual revenue.

Before our checkout optimization project, Kimirica suffered from a high **68% cart abandonment rate**. Analytics indicated that users were adding items to their cart but dropping out immediately when faced with shipping charge surprises, mandatory account registrations, and slow loading forms.

Goals & Objectives:

  • Increase overall platform conversion rate by 12% to 18% through user discovery improvements.
  • Accelerate checkout flow completion time by 20% by introducing guest checkout.
  • Minimize cart drop-offs by revealing shipping fees early in the user journey.

Target Audience

Our primary e-commerce customers fall into:

Gifting Customers (45%)

Users buying premium sets for birthdays or festivals. They value custom greetings, premium packaging options, and guaranteed delivery timelines.

Self-Care Shoppers (55%)

Repeat customers purchasing personal products. They value fast checkouts, personalized recommendations, and active order trackers.

3. Research & Discovery

We set up heatmaps (Hotjar) and ran remote interviews with 12 frequent online shoppers to understand funnel barriers.

Funnel Loss Audit

Journey Stage Completion Rate (Before) Primary Drop-Off Reason
Product View → Add to Cart 42% (Normal) Price comparison, sizing queries
Cart drawer → Enter Details 28% (Poor) Hidden delivery charges shown late, forced registration
Payment Gateway Success 54% (Low) Slow OTP inputs, lack of express UPI checkout options

User Pain Points

01
Surprise Shipping Fees

Shipping charges were calculated at the very last payment screen, causing instant user anger and cart abandonment.

02
Forced Account Creation

Forcing users to create accounts, verify passwords, and validate emails before buying a simple soap took 3+ minutes.

03
Poor Coupon Usability

Applying coupon codes required manual copying. When codes failed without explanation, users left to search for deals elsewhere.

Product Thinking Insight:

We realized users shopping online have a high baseline expectation set by Amazon and Shopify checkout speeds. If a premium D2C platform takes more than 4 clicks to pay, it feels dated. We had to implement an Express Checkout strategy.

4. Problem Definition

The Challenges & Constraints

We had to balance high D2C branding requirements (showing beautiful lifestyle visuals and up-sell gifting boxes) with high checkout speeds.

+15% Target checkout speed
< 3 mins Target purchase duration
-15% Cart abandonment reduction

5. Design Process

We mapped out a 3-step checkout flow (Cart → Shipping → Payment) and introduced a "Slide-out Express Cart Drawer" that allowed checkout without reloading.

Fig 1. User flow transition from detailed shopping catalog to cart details.

Low-Fidelity Iterations

We iterated heavily on the "Promo Code Selector" UI. In early sketches, the discount input field was hidden behind an accordion menu. Users rarely clicked it, causing them to abandon the cart to search for codes. We redesigned it into a visible modal showcasing active deals.

Fig 2. Iterations optimizing the promo code modal container on mobile viewports.

6. Visual Design System

To reinforce luxury brand consistency, we upgraded the typography guidelines and visual design buttons to support high contrast, elegant frames, and clean input lines.

Fig 3. E-commerce UI components library, typography, and card tokens.

7. High-Fidelity Walkthrough

The final design implements:

Slide-out Interactive Cart Drawer

Users can verify items, choose shipping method, select gift wrapping options, and see exact delivery times directly in the cart drawer.

Single-Click Payment Integrations

We integrated Apple Pay, Google Pay, and major UPI providers at the first stage of the checkout drawer, bypassing 3 pages of details.

8. Product Strategy & Trade-offs

We chose to make "Guest Checkout" the default journey. The marketing team initially worried about losing customer data. We solved this by offering a 10% coupon *after* checkout on the confirmation page if they saved their password, converting 42% of guest checkouts into accounts without funnel friction.

9. Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Complex Gifting Address Handling

The Problem: Gifting orders required shipping to multiple addresses, but the original interface made users place separate orders, driving up shipping costs and frustration.

Design Solution: We created a "Send as Gift" option allowing custom messaging cards and address split workflows in the same transaction loop.
