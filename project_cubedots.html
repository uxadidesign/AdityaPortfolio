1. Project Overview

Background & Business Context

Cubedots is a real estate transaction SaaS platform connecting builders, brokers, and buyers. Real estate is high-value and slow-moving, requiring extensive compliance review and high-density financial contract tracking.

Before our redesign project, Cubedots users struggled with an outdated dashboard designed around separate data tables. Real estate agents could not get a unified status overview of their current listings, and developers wasted over 20% of their sprints rebuild ad-hoc buttons and input states.

Goals & Objectives:

  • Reduce task error rates by 15% through clean information architecture and visual prioritization.
  • Speed up developer onboarding and eliminate rework by building standard Figma tokens.
  • Accelerate overall client transaction cycles by 25%.

Target Audience

Our primary dashboard audience:

Real Estate Agents (70%)

Power users processing transactions, uploading customer contracts, and monitoring pricing status updates. They value speed, hotkeys, and data density.

Property Developers (30%)

Enterprise clients managing pricing configurations and listing allocations. They value clear reporting graphs and csv import logs.

2. My Role & Collaboration

As the Lead Designer, I owned the layout structures, high-density data table guidelines, and component specifications inside Figma.

I worked alongside:

  • Product Managers: Mapping out complex compliance approval gates and paperwork validations.
  • Engineering team: Standardizing spacing variables and button states inside a shared design library.
  • QA engineers: Creating checks for edge-case dashboard table scenarios (overflow text, extreme values, loading state blocks).

3. Research & Discovery

We ran contextual inquiries with 8 real estate agents, shadowing their typical daily routines as they managed client profiles.

Contextual Audit Pain Points

01
Tab Overload

Agents kept 10+ browser tabs open because they couldn't compare multiple listings or transaction histories in a single screen.

02
Visual Distraction

Primary action buttons (Approve Contract, Archive Listing) had identical gray styling, leading to accidental deletions.

03
Dev-Design Mismatch

Developers coded pages by eyeing mockups, leading to inconsistent font-sizes and input fields that broke accessibility guidelines.

Product Thinking Insight:

We realized that SaaS layouts are productivity tools. Users don't log in to admire beautiful spacing; they log in to finish a task. Every extra click is wasted business cost. We had to focus on visual prioritization and high-density layouts.

8. Product Strategy & Trade-offs

We traded fancy visual animations for speed. High-volume data loading is heavily throttled by custom animation scripts. We chose to implement a native web layout structure that loaded instantly, prioritizing speed over visual styling.

9. Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Standardizing Complex Financial Formulas

The Problem: Property pricing involved complex calculations for broker payouts, VAT structures, and progressive commissions, leading to manual accounting errors.

Design Solution: We created an interactive calculator with real-time feedback that computed margins inline, reducing input errors by 15%.
