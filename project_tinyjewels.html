1. Overview

Tiny Jewels, a premium D2C jewelry brand, brought me on as a freelance designer to rebuild their storefront ahead of a festive sales push. Jewelry is a category where the average order value is high and the purchase is entirely sight-unseen — the product page has to do all the reassuring work a customer would normally get from holding the piece in a store.

2. Problem

The existing product pages showed a handful of static photos with no way to inspect detail, no visible certification or authenticity information, and no clarity on how a high-value item would be shipped safely — leaving hesitant first-time buyers to abandon their cart rather than commit to a purchase they couldn't fully evaluate.

Customer interviews confirmed the hesitation wasn't about price — it was about not being able to verify quality and trust the shipping process for something this valuable.

3. Solution

I redesigned the product page around trust signals a jeweler would normally provide in person — certification badges, true-to-scale sizing context, and 360° detail zoom — paired with transparent, insured-shipping messaging visible before checkout, not buried in a policy page.

Fig 1. Redesigned product page with certification badges, 360° zoom, and insured-shipping messaging.

4. My Role

As the sole designer on a small freelance engagement, I ran quick customer interviews myself, designed the product page and checkout trust signals, and worked directly with the founder and a part-time developer to ship within a tight five-week timeline before the festive season.

5. Key Features

Certification & Authenticity Badges

Hallmark and certification details surfaced directly on the product page rather than a separate policy document, addressing the top trust concern head-on.

360° Detail Zoom

An interactive zoom view replacing static photos, letting customers inspect craftsmanship detail the way they would under a jeweler's loupe.

Insured Shipping Transparency

Clear, upfront messaging about insured, tracked delivery shown at the product page and cart, not discovered only after checkout.

6. Business Impact

Higher Add-to-Cart Rate
Improved Average Order Value
Fewer Pre-Purchase Support Queries
